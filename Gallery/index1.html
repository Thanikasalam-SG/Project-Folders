Ducks

Ducks are an immense group of aquatic birds, known as waterfowl. They are in the Anatidae family, and share this family with swans and geese as well

Dolphins

Dolphins are part of the toothed whales. Generally, they are among the smaller whales. Most live in salt water oceans, but some live in rivers - there are oceanic dolphins and river dolphins.

Bulls

An adult male is known as a bull. Many male cattle are castrated to reduce their aggressive tendencies and make them more tractable.

Deer

A deer or true deer is a hoofed ruminant ungulate of the family Cervidae. Cervidae is divided into subfamilies Cervinae and Capreolinae.

White Pigs

The Large White is a British breed of domestic pig. It derives from the old Large Yorkshire breed from the county of Yorkshire, in northern England.

Fox

Foxes are the smallest members of the dog family Canidae. The fox has pointed ears, narrow snout, and a bushy tail.

Wolf

wolf, any of two species of wild doglike carnivores. The gray, or timber, wolf (Canis lupus) is the better known.

Cat

Cats are quiet and well-behaved animals, making them popular pets. Young kittens are playful.

Lions

The lion is a wild animal. The jungle is its natural habitat. It is called “the King of the Jungle” because of its massive size and raw power.

Lions

The lion is famous for its roar and hunting ability. It lives in a ‘pride.’

Whale

The killer whale is one of the most recognizable marine mammals, namely due to their black and white pattern, and large size.

Crocodile

Crocodiles or true crocodiles are large semiaquatic reptiles that live throughout the tropics in Africa, Asia, the Americas and Australia

Dragonfly

Dragonfly species (Anisoptera) are characterized by long bodies with two narrow pairs of intricately veined, membranous wings

Crab

Crabs are members of the animal group called crustaceans. There are at least 7,000 species, or kinds, of crab.

Peacock

Peacocks are extravagant birds, with iridescent blue necks, and bright green tail feathers dotted with eyespots.
